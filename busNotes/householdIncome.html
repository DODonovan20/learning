Income refers to all wealth received over a period of time.
Income generates a taxation responsibility. (liability)

Benefits in Kind (BIK) refers to all sources of non-money income recieved by households and individuals. They are sometimes called perks.
E.g. free travel, company car.

    Major Sources of Income

  • Wages and Salaries: income from employment.

  • Pensions: paid to those who have retired from work.

  • Child Benefit: a payment made to parents of children.

  • Jobseeker's Benefit: a paymrnt to those who are unable to find employment.

  • Family Income Supplement: a top up payment to haouseholds with low levels of income.

  • Interest on Savings: a reward for saving money in banks, credit unions, etc.

  • Dividends on Shares: shareholders are owner of a company. They may be rewarded for investment by a portion of company profits called a dividend.

  • Rental Income: income received from rent on propeties.

Regular Vs Irregular Income

Regular income is recieved on a regular basis and the timing and amounts tend to be predictable.
E.g. wages, salaries, pension.

Irregular income is far less predictable and will only be recieved from time to time. There is also less certainty about the amounts.
E.g. overtime, bonus, lottery win.

Income From Employment

Salary: fixed annual payment made to an employee. Usually paid in monthly instalments.

Wage: a payment recieved for work done. it is normally calculated on the basis of actual work completed, or on the time spent working. Wages are usually paid weekly and are likely to vary. Wages can be calculated using a number of different methods:

  1. Time Rate: wages calculated based on the number of hours worked.

  2. Peice Rate: wages calculated based on the nuber of items made or tasks completed.

  3. Commisions: sales staff may be paid a percentage of the value of the goods and services they sell.

  4. Overtime: involves an extra payment to employees who work extra hours above and beyond their standard working week. These extra hours are usually rewarded with a higher than normal rate of pay. For example, double time or time and a half.

  5. Bonus: an extra payment for reaching an agreed upon preformance or deadline.

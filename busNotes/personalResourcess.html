A resource is anything that we can use to meet our needs or our goals. e.g. material, goods, knowledge and time.
Resources: help us achive things, are useful or valuable, and are often limited or scarce.

Money is anything of value that is accepted by people in exchange of goods and services.

Prioritising our spending helps us make informed and balanced decisions about what to spending money on. Money should be soent on essential items first.

Finacial literacy - the skills needed to be money smart.

Types of Resources:

>

  1. Physical/Capital rsources: - Goods that are made by people and which can be used to provide other goods or services. E.g. buildings, property, computers , equipment, phones, books.

  2. Natural Rescources: - Households may have access to goods provided by nature. E.g. land water

  3. Financial Resources: - Includes all types of money available to households or individuals They allow people to access goods/services. E.g. employment income, child benefit, savings

  4. Human Rescources: - Refers to your skills, abilities and experience. It also includes all the other peole who are available to help you. E.g. abiltity to: read, write, solve problems, family, friends, teachers.

  5. Time Resources: - These are a valuable resource. It our most limited resource and you cannot earn, borrow, or buy more time.

Needs Vs Wants

A need is somethig we can't do without or something that p;ays a very important part of daily life. E.g. food, water, clothes. shelter

A want is something we would really like to have. It may improve daily life but is not essential. E.g. sports car, designer clothes, new phone

The 2 types of costs

  1. Financial cost - this is the cost of what you chose buy.

  2. Opportunity cost - this is the next best thing you could buy.

E.g. If you spend $10 on a t-shirt you wont have the money to buy phone credit. The finacial cost was $10 and the opportunity cost was the phone credit.
